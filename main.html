About Me

My name is Koen Van Der Hoeven and I am currently a student studying Computer Science at the University of Pennsylvania. Nationalitywise I am from the Netherlands. I am currently 20 years old and I have spent my life exploring multiple cultures living in many countries. On the list include Singapore, Thailand, Vietnam, Ireland, France and the United States.

When it comes to academic interests the list can go on forever, whether it be History, Mathematics, Physics or Computer Science there is little that does not gauge my interest. My passion however lies with Computation, hence driving me towards my selection in majoring in Computer Science, minoring in Mathematics. Computer Science is a an expansive field, practically anything I can think about uses computation in some way or another. To me Computer Science allows me to meld theory together with practicality to create technical and computational solutions to everyday problems. The interdisciplinary side of Computer Science allured me to study the major now allowing me to persue my goal of becoming a software developer. I hope to see myself in a future position where using the skills I have learnt throughout to work together in teams and manage others to create solutions to problems that will aid the general public, at the same time working alongside others to help advance the field forward.

Despite being interested in the broad subject of Computer Science I have a deep interest in studying about graph theory and robotics, with both subtopics interweaving between each other. Over the coming years of my academia I plan to tailor my studies to strengthen my knowledge on both of these fields.
